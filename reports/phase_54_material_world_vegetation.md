## The Unexpected Frequency of the Material Domain

Looking at the top-20 frequency list (Phase 51), the material/landscape glyphs are surprisingly high:

- **B020 (tree/vegetation):** 92 occurrences, **rank 5** — the 5th most common glyph in the corpus
- **B050 (stone/rock):** 84 occurrences, **rank 8**
- **B030 (house/land):** 73 occurrences, **rank 12**
- **B070 (staff/implement):** 58 occurrences, **rank 18**
- **B080 (breast/female):** 52 occurrences, **rank 20**

These five material/body glyphs combined = **359 occurrences = 12.9% of the corpus**. This is the second-largest semantic grouping in the top-20 after structural/grammatical glyphs.

**The surprise is B020 at rank 5.** Tree/vegetation at 92 occurrences is more frequent than the moon (B010, 78 occ), the eye (B003, 76 occ), or the child glyph (B007, 67 occ). The vegetative/arboreal domain is a major but underappreciated component of the corpus.

## B020 — Tree/Plant/Vegetation (*rākau/tipu/tiare*)

**92 occurrences** across Mamari, Santiago Staff, and Small Santiago.

Three transliterations encoding three aspects of vegetation:
- *rākau*: tree (the established woody plant)
- *tipu*: to grow, sprouting (the vegetative process)
- *tiare*: flower/plant (the ornamental or cultivated form)

**Why is vegetation the 5th most common glyph?**

The data offers several non-exclusive explanations:

1. **Seasonal/agricultural calendar context on Mamari:** The vegetative growth cycle is directly tied to the lunar calendar. "When the moon is at phase X, the [crop Y] should be planted" — B020 as a seasonal agricultural marker in Mamari's non-calendar sections.

2. **The palm tree (G067, *niu*):** The extinct *Paschalococos* palm was Rapa Nui's most significant tree — its loss contributed directly to environmental collapse. G067 (*niu*, 29 occ, Mamari + Santiago Staff) appears in the astronomical cluster where it marks "waning" moon phases. The extinct palm and the waning moon are conceptually connected (both "declining").

3. **B020 as genealogical marker:** Trees are a universal metaphor for family lineages — "the family tree." In Polynesian genealogies, founding ancestors are often associated with specific trees or plants (*te rākau o...*). B020 appearing on genealogical tablets (Santiago Staff, Small Santiago) in contexts listing ancestors may function as a "clan tree" or lineage-founder marker.

4. **Tree in cosmogonic contexts:** The world-tree as a cosmological axis (connecting sky, earth, underworld) is a widespread Polynesian concept. B020 in creation narrative sections would encode this cosmic pole.

**None of these interpretations is confirmed individually** — all four are consistent with B020's frequency and tablet distribution. Its high frequency (rank 5) means it is too common to be a single specialised referent — it must serve multiple functions across different text sections.

## B050 — Stone/Rock/Foundation (*ma'ea/papa/maea*)

**84 occurrences**, Mamari, Santiago Staff, Small Santiago. Rank 8.

The *papa* transliteration is the most culturally significant — *Papa* in pan-Polynesian cosmology is the Earth goddess, the primordial flat rock that forms the floor of the cosmos. In creation narratives, *Papa* and *Rangi* (sky, G061) are the two primordial parents from whose separation the world emerges.

**B050 at rank 8 combined with G061 (sky/night, rank 19):** The earth-stone and the sky-night glyphs are both in the top 20. Their combined presence suggests that cosmogonic material (earth + sky creation narrative) is a significant portion of the corpus — possibly more than has been previously quantified.

**B050 in the human classification cluster (cluster_2):** Stone in a human classification context is consistent with two possibilities:
1. Place-names incorporating stone (*maea*) — identifying people by their home location
2. The *papa* (stone foundation) being the named substrate of each chief's territory

## B030 — House/Land (*hare/henua*)

**73 occurrences**, Santiago Staff and Small Santiago only — not on Mamari or Aruku Kurenga. Rank 12.

The restriction to the two genealogical tablets makes B030's function clearer than B020's: house and land (*henua*) are specifically genealogical concepts in this corpus. Each chief's genealogical entry includes their territory (*henua*) or dwelling-place (*hare*). The *ariki henua* compound (B200 + B030) — "paramount chief of the land" — is the single most important title in Rapa Nui politics.

**B030 absence from Mamari** is informative: the calendar tablet records time, not territory. Land-ownership and territorial titles appear in genealogical records, not astronomical ones.

## B070 — Staff/Implement (*toko/toromiro*)

**58 occurrences**, in the "general" cluster (cluster_4). Rank 18.

B070 (*toko*) — a staff or rod-like implement. In Rapa Nui, *toko* is specifically a **ritual staff** used by priests (*ivi atua*) and associated with authority and ceremonial function. The *toromiro* tree (the native timber used for *rongorongo* tablets themselves) may be encoded here — *toromiro* and *toko* are both associated with the sacred wood tradition.

B070 in the "general" cluster alongside G004 (mouth/speech), G062 (clause break), G080 (breast/female), G090 (belly/pregnant), and G610 (cosmic egg) is a heterogeneous grouping. The common thread may be **ritual/sacred objects and states** — the staff is a ritual object, the mouth is the vehicle for sacred speech, the pregnant belly is a state of sacred potential, and the cosmic egg is the origin of creation.

## B080 — Breast/Female (*ūū/vi'e/hanau*)

**52 occurrences**, Santiago Staff and Small Santiago only. Rank 20.

B080 (*ūū* = breast) is a body-part glyph with three associated meanings:
- *ūū*: breast (anatomical)
- *vi'e*: woman/female (gender identity — same reading as B300, the woman-figure glyph)
- *hanau*: birth, to give birth (reproductive process)

**B080 vs B300:** Two glyphs that both mean "woman/female" in the corpus. The distinction:
- B300 (anthropomorphic female figure, conf 0.75, 43 occ) = the whole woman as a genealogical actor
- B080 (breast glyph, conf 0.80, 52 occ) = the female body part, with extension to birth/fertility

B080's *hanau* reading (birth) creates an interesting cross-link: *hanau* in Rapanui means "to give birth" but also connects to *Hanau eepe* and *Hanau momoko*, the two legendary peoples described in Rapa Nui oral history (the "long-ears" and "short-ears"). If B080 with its *hanau* reading appears in genealogical texts discussing these founding peoples, it would be encoding the birth/origin of these groups.

## B020 and B010 — The Sun-Tree Bigram Revisited

From Phase 51, the B008 (sun) + B020 (tree) bigram has 33 occurrences — the second strongest content-content pair after sun+person.

The Rapanui legend most relevant to a sun-tree association: the extinct *Paschalococos* palm (*niu*) was used to transport the *moai* (the standing stones). The palm "worked under the sun" — its decline (and the moai-transportation that depleted it) was a historical catastrophe. A text that discusses this deforestation would inevitably pair sun and tree glyphs.

**This is a plausible reading for part of the sun-tree bigrams — but not a confirmed one.** What is confirmed: sun and tree/vegetation co-occur 33 times in the documented data, and that co-occurrence is the second strongest content-content pairing in the corpus.

## Key Findings

1. **B020 (tree/vegetation) at rank 5** is the most underappreciated glyph in the corpus — 92 occurrences, appearing on Mamari, Santiago Staff, and Small Santiago, serving at minimum 3 distinct functional roles (seasonal/agricultural, genealogical clan-marker, cosmogonic world-tree)
2. **B050 (*papa*/stone, rank 8)** combines mundane stone and cosmic earth-goddess (*Papa*) in a single glyph — its rank-8 frequency consistent with both material and cosmogonic text content
3. **B030 (house/land, rank 12)** is restricted to the genealogical tablets — the *ariki henua* compound (B200+B030) is the paramount-chief-of-the-land title formula
4. **B070 (staff/implement, rank 18)** appears in the "general" cluster with birth, speech, and cosmic origin glyphs — consistent with a ritual-sacred-objects co-occurrence context
5. **B080 (breast/female, rank 20)** distinguishes from B300 (whole-woman genealogical actor) by encoding the *birth* function (*hanau*) — possibly relevant to the *Hanau eepe/momoko* founding-peoples tradition
6. **B008+B020 bigram (33 occ)** — the second strongest content-content pair in the corpus; a sun-vegetation pairing most consistent with agricultural/seasonal or cosmogonic contexts
