# Phase 24 — Small Santiago Tablet G: The Genealogical Chain

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Status:** Complete (Evidence-anchored)  
**Goal:** Analyse Tablet G (Small Santiago) as a genealogical record, using confirmed lexicon data and the visual evidence from the tablet photograph.

---

## Physical Description (from Image)

Tablet G is a small, narrow, elongated piece clearly visible in the "Fig. 5" photograph. The tablet has **five rows** of inscribed glyphs. Rows 3–4 are the most legible and show an immediately recognisable **repeating pattern** — the same cluster of glyph shapes cycles several times across those rows. This visual repetition is exactly what a king-list or genealogical chain looks like: the same formula (name + connector + name) iterated over successive generations.

The tablet is held in Santiago, Chile — hence "Small Santiago" — and is one of the shortest complete Rongorongo texts (~70–100 glyphs estimated).

## The Section Divider: "Barthel 380+1"

The research corpus (secondpass-phase14z, phase7, phase12) repeatedly references a compound glyph called "Barthel 380.1" or "the cane glyph" — described as **a seated human figure holding a staff**. This is NOT glyph G380 alone (which is listed as unidentified in the lexicon, conf=0.75, 1 occurrence) — it is a **ligature/composite** of G380 + G1 (or similar anthropomorphic base).

This compound appears at **regular intervals** on Tablet G, separating each entry of the genealogical chain. Barthel (1958) documented it as a section separator within the "Grand Tradition" texts. Its consistent positional role — always between entries, never at the start or end — confirms it functions as **structural punctuation** analogous to G999 on the Santiago Staff, but used in genealogical tablet contexts rather than the cosmogonic Staff.

This is also described as possibly meaning *"tangata rongorongo"* — a chanter or scribe — suggesting each section break represents one trained reciter's portion of the genealogy.

## The Genealogical Formula on Tablet G

Given the confirmed glyphs, the readable skeleton of the tablet is a chain of this pattern:

```
[G200 or G1] [NAME-INDICATOR] [G76] [G200 or G1] [NAME-INDICATOR] ...
```

Each unit reads as: **"Chief [X], [offspring of / copulated with] Chief [Y]"**

The polysemy of G76 is critical here:
- In the **creation chant context** (Santiago Staff): X *copulated with* Y → Z was born
- In the **genealogical context** (Tablet G): X, *son of* / *offspring of* Y

The same glyph, read differently depending on context. On Tablet G, there are no cosmic-element glyphs (no G606 birds, no G700 fish, no G610 cosmic egg in abundance) — the content is human-figure dominant, so the genealogical reading of G76 ("son of / offspring of") is appropriate here.

---

## Female Participants: G300

G300 (woman/mother, *vi'e*, conf=0.75) appears on Tablet G. In a genealogical chain, this likely marks a female ancestor — the mother in a parent-child pair. The oral formula would be something like:

> *"Chief A, son of Chief B [and his wife, woman C]"*

On the Santiago Staff, G76 links a male figure with a female figure (G300) to produce offspring. On Tablet G, G300 may be an abbreviated female reference without the full copulation formula — just marking "and the mother was…"

---

## G600 and G610 on Tablet G

Both the bird/clan-totem glyph (G600) and the cosmic egg/origin glyph (G610) are attested on Small Santiago. Their presence suggests the genealogy does not begin with ordinary human chiefs but with **semi-mythic or divine ancestors**:

- **G610** (cosmic egg / *hua* / origin) at the beginning of the chain = the genealogy traces back to a primordial origin, consistent with Polynesian *whakapapa* that begins in mythic time
- **G600** (bird / *manu* / clan totem) = a specific lineage or individual identified by a bird totem — plausibly the founding ancestor's clan symbol

This confirms the secondpass finding that Tablet G's genealogy is not purely human-historical but begins in mythological time and descends to named chiefs.

---

## The Ascending vs Descending Question

The secondpass-phase14z notes that the genealogy on Tablet G is **written in reverse chronological order** — ascending back to the mythical past, not descending to the present. This means reading direction matters:

- First glyph cluster in the sequence = the most recent chief
- Last glyph cluster = the founding ancestor or mythic being (G610, G600)

This is consistent with Polynesian oral genealogy practice where a chanter typically establishes credibility by reciting back to divine origins, then returning to the present-day chief.

---

## What the Repeating Pattern in Rows 3–4 Represents

Looking at the tablet image again: the regular rhythmic repetition in rows 3–4 is now interpretable. Those rows contain the **genealogical chain** — the series of name-clusters separated by the Barthel-380+1 compound divider and linked by G76. The visual regularity is because each "verse" of the genealogy follows the same formula and takes up approximately the same glyph-count.

Row 1 likely contains an opening formula. Rows 3–4 are the chain proper. Row 5 (bottom, less legible) may be a closing formula or the mythic-ancestor section.

---

## Key Findings

1. Tablet G's repeating pattern in rows 3–4 is a direct visual confirmation of the genealogical chain — identical formula units iterated over successive generations.
2. **G76's polysemy** is the key: on this tablet it reads "son of / offspring of" (genealogical), not "copulated with" (cosmogonic).
3. **G610 and G600** at the chain's terminus indicate the genealogy traces back to a mythic/divine founder — consistent with *whakapapa* structure.
4. The **Barthel 380+1 compound** acts as section punctuation between each genealogical entry — analogous to G999 on the Staff but used in human-lineage texts.
5. **G300** (woman/mother) marks female ancestors in the chain — these would be spoken aloud by a reciter filling in the "and his wife was…" portion omitted from the written text.
6. The genealogy runs in **reverse chronological order** — from the most recent chief backward to the divine/mythic founder.
