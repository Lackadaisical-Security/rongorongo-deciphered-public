## The Two Zones of Mamari

These 19 glyphs cluster into two distinct functional zones that the tablet contains:

### Zone A: The Lunar Calendar Section (Lines C6–C8)
**Glyphs primarily active:** G10, G12, G40, G62, G67, G69, G74, G78, G143, G152, G280 + G8 (temporal), G20 (agricultural layer)

The calendar section encodes the 30-night lunar month. Working from the confirmed glyphs:

```
OPENING:
G69 (lizard / Hiro) = New moon — Hiro's night — cycle begins

WAXING PHASE:
G10 (crescent) = General moon marker, repeated through waxing nights
G12 (fish) = Upright orientation = moon waxing / growing

FIRST QUARTER:
G74 (Hua / fruit) = Night ~8 — first quarter moon

APPROACHING FULL:
G143 (Rākau / tree) = Nights ~11–12 — penultimate pre-full nights

FULL MOON:
G152 (Omotohi) = Night 15 — FULL MOON — structural anchor

WANING BEGINS:
G78 (Maure) = Night ~16 — first waning night
G12 (fish) = INVERTED orientation from here — moon waning
G67 (extinct palm) = Waning half label — Rongo's phase

DARK MOON:
G280 (turtle / honu) = Nights ~26–28 — dark moon / Rongo

NEXT CYCLE OPENS:
G69 (lizard) again? — new Hiro night marks month restart
```

**Agricultural overlay:** G20 (grow/*tipu*) and G8 (sun/light/temporal) are woven through the calendar sections marking:
- Planting phase (waxing, pre-first-quarter)
- Growth phase (waxing, first-quarter to full moon)
- Harvest phase (waning, post-full)

**Connectivity:** G62 (*ki* / "and then") chains consecutive nights together. G40 (water/flow) appears in the Kokore sections as a flow/repetition marker for the unnamed nights.

### Zone B: Non-Calendar Sections
**Glyphs primarily active:** G1, G6, G76, G200, G600, G606 + G8 (celestial)

Mamari contains **genealogical content** outside the calendar section. This is confirmed by:
- G76 (beget, conf=0.95) on Mamari — genealogical connector
- G200 (ariki, conf=0.95) on Mamari — chief/title
- G1 (person, conf=0.85) on Mamari — ancestors
- G600/G606 (bird/spirits, conf=0.95) on Mamari — clan totems or cosmogonic references

These glyphs are **not part of the lunar calendar** (which uses G69, G74, G143, G152, G78, G280). They appear in other sections of the tablet, possibly:
- A brief creation/cosmogony passage
- A short genealogical record of a ruling lineage
- Ritual invocations connected to the calendar (invoking the lineage that controls the calendar knowledge)

## What Mamari Is

Drawing the two zones together: **Mamari is not solely a calendar tablet.** It is a tablet where:

1. A **lunar almanac** (Zone A) provides the operational timekeeping framework
2. A **genealogical/cosmogonic section** (Zone B) provides the mythological authority behind that framework — the lineage of chiefs or gods who own the calendar knowledge

This is consistent with how Polynesian sacred knowledge works: you cannot teach someone the calendar without also telling them *whose* calendar it is, and *who* authorised you to read it. The genealogy and the calendar are inseparable.

## The G69 / G152 / G280 Triad

The three highest-confidence non-basic glyphs on Mamari form a **symbolic triad** that brackets the lunar month:

| Glyph | Night | Meaning | Symbolic role |
|-------|-------|---------|--------------|
| G69 | Opening | Lizard / Hiro / new moon | Birth — the month begins |
| G152 | Night 15 | Omotohi / full moon | Completion — the month's peak |
| G280 | Closing | Turtle / Rongo / dark moon | Death and rest — the month ends |

Birth → Completion → Rest → (Birth again). This is the lunar cycle as a life cycle, encoded in three animal glyphs. The lizard that sheds its skin, the full moon in all its brightness, the turtle that dives beneath the surface. Hiro's rain, the Old Woman's fire, Rongo's deep ocean.

## Key Findings

1. **19 glyphs confirmed on Mamari** — the most complete glyph inventory for any single tablet in this analysis.
2. Mamari has **two functional zones**: the lunar calendar (G69, G74, G143, G152, G78, G280, G12, G67) and the genealogical/cosmogonic sections (G76, G200, G1, G600, G606).
3. The **G69 / G152 / G280 triad** forms a symbolic arc across the month: new moon (birth) → full moon (completion) → dark moon (rest).
4. G20 (grow/*tipu*) and G62 (*ki*) serve as the **agricultural and sequential scaffold** for the calendar section.
5. The calendar and the genealogy are not separate texts — they are two components of a single sacred document: the *almanac-with-authority*.
