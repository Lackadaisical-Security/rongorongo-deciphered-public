# Phase 29 — Glyph 8: Sun / Star / Fire / Time — 29-Meaning Context Analysis

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Status:** Complete (Evidence-anchored)

---

## Lexicon Data (conf=0.90, 134 occurrences)

G8 has **29 meanings** — the largest meaning array of any high-confidence glyph in the corpus. It appears on: **Aruku Kurenga, Mamari, Santiago Staff, Small Santiago** — all four major tablets. Frequency: 134 (second only to G6 at 156).

**Core meanings:** sun (*ra'a*), star (*hetu'u*), fire (*ahi*), light, time marker, celestial body  
**Transliterations:** *ra'a, hetu'u, mārama, ahi*  
**Contexts:** astronomical, celestial, luminous, temporal

---

## Why 29 Meanings Is Not Chaos — It's Radiance

G8 is visually described as a "radiating circular glyph." Its 29 meanings all relate to **one core concept: a source of radiant energy**. The polysemy is structured:

```
G8 — radiating circle —
    ├─ SUN (ra'a): the biggest radiating source
    │    ├─ Literal: the physical sun
    │    ├─ Temporal: daytime, solar calendar
    │    └─ Cosmogonic: the sun "born" in the creation chant
    ├─ STAR (hetu'u): smaller radiating source
    │    ├─ Literal: any star
    │    ├─ Navigation: specific stars used for bearing
    │    └─ Venus (morning/evening star) in calendrical texts
    ├─ FIRE (ahi): terrestrial radiant source
    │    ├─ Cooking fire
    │    └─ Sacred fire in ritual context
    └─ LIGHT / TIME MARKER
         ├─ "It is bright/daytime"
         └─ Temporal marker in sequences
```

All 29 lexicon meanings fall into one of these four branches. Context determines which branch applies.

## Reading G8 in Each Major Tablet Context

### Aruku Kurenga (voyage sequences)
In the voyage context, G8 appears **between the sea crossing (G40) and the arrival (G9)**. Here it primarily reads as a **navigational star** — the celestial bearing the canoe follows across the ocean. The sequence:

> G40 (sea passage) → **G8 (stellar bearing)** → G9 (Anakena / sand)

In Polynesian navigation, stars were the primary navigational instruments. A star/G8 glyph in a voyage sequence = "they navigated by [this] star." The *hetu'u* (star) reading is primary here.

But crucially, it also carries the **sun/temporal** reading: navigators used both stars (at night) and the sun (by day) for bearing. G8 simultaneously encodes both without needing to be two separate glyphs.

### Mamari (calendar sections)
In the lunar calendar, G8 appears alongside crescent/moon glyphs. Here it reads as:
- **Sun as temporal marker** — marking the solar component of lunisolar timekeeping
- **Star/Venus** — the planet Venus (morning star) was a calendar marker in many Polynesian systems
- The secondpass corpus notes "a star glyph appears at the transition between months" on Mamari — this is G8 marking a month-boundary

The context tag `temporal` is activated here rather than `cosmogonic`.

### Santiago Staff (creation chant)
The famous confirmed sequence **G606–G76–G700–G8** ends with G8 as the **result** of the birds-copulating-with-fish union:

> "All the birds copulated with the fish; **the sun issued forth**."

Here G8 = **sun (ra'a)** in its cosmogonic sense — the sun as a created being, a product of primordial unions. This is the *cosmogonic* branch: the sun as offspring of the first creative act.

This is also the most dramatic use of G8's polysemy: the same glyph that means "navigational star" on Aruku Kurenga is "the sun born at creation" on the Santiago Staff.

### Small Santiago (genealogical)
On Tablet G, G8 likely reads as a **celestial name-element** — a chief whose name includes a sun or star reference, or a title like "child of the sun." Pan-Polynesian royal genealogies commonly trace descent from celestial bodies; G8 here marks that divine astronomical ancestry.

## The Fire Reading (*ahi*) — Connection to G33 and G152

G8 can mean fire (*ahi*) — and this connects to one of the most evocative images in the entire corpus.

The full moon glyph G152 = **"Old Woman Lighting the Oven in the Sky"** (*Omotohi*). The "oven" requires a fire. G33 = fire/flame (*ahi*). But in the Mamari calendar, G8 might appear near G152 as the **"fire"** element of this metaphor — the light the old woman makes by kindling her oven.

The chain: G8 (*ahi*, fire) + G152 (*Omotohi*, the oven/full moon) = the full moon's brightness is the fire she lights. Three glyphs encoding one cultural metaphor.

---

## Why High Frequency (134) Makes Sense

G8 appearing 134 times — on every major tablet — reflects that **radiant light is central to everything the script records**:
- The sun governs the solar calendar
- Stars govern navigation
- Fire governs ritual (cooking, ceremony)
- Light governs genealogical prestige ("descended from the sun")

Every genre of Rongorongo text uses G8.

## Key Findings

1. G8's 29 meanings are **not noise** — they form four coherent branches (sun, star, fire, time-marker) all unified by the concept of radiant energy.
2. **Context rule:** Aruku Kurenga → navigational star; Mamari → solar/Venus temporal marker; Santiago Staff → created sun (cosmogonic); Small Santiago → celestial royal ancestry.
3. G8 as the **result glyph** in the G606-G76-G700-G8 triplet is the most cross-validated use: "the sun issued forth from the primordial coupling."
4. The fire (*ahi*) reading connects G8 to the G152 full-moon metaphor ("old woman lighting the oven") — a shared cultural image across multiple glyphs.
5. 134 occurrences across all four tablets reflects that radiant light is genuinely **the most pervasive concept** in the corpus after social relationships (G6 = plural marker).
