## The Three-Sequence Structure

The triple repetition on Tablet B was first noted by Butinov & Knorozov (1956) and is thoroughly documented in the research corpus. The three sequences correspond to:

1. **Sequence I** — Hau-Maka's spirit-voyage of reconnaissance  
2. **Sequence II** — The seven young scouts dispatched by the king  
3. **Sequence III** — Hotu Matuʻa's great migration and settlement

**G32 (section start/delimiter)** is confirmed on Aruku Kurenga in the master lexicon — its role as separator between the three sequences is consistent with its secondpass-phase12 description: "On Aruku Kurenga marks start of each voyage sequence."

---

## What Can Be Confirmed About Each Sequence

### All Three Sequences Share

- **G40 (water/sea)** — sea crossing is encoded in every sequence
- **G8 (sun/star)** — celestial navigation reference appears in voyage context across sequences  
- **G9 (sand/Anakena)** — **the terminal glyph** — this is the single most important confirmed detail: all three sequences end at the sand (Anakena). This was verified by Metoro's reading of a similar glyph as *one* (sand) and by the oral tradition identifying Anakena as the settlement landing point

### Sequence II Specifically

**G7+G6** (child + plural/hand) = "the young ones [plural]" — the scouts as a group of youths. This is directly supported by:
- Lexicon: G7 = "child / descendant (*poki*)", conf=0.80, on Aruku Kurenga
- Lexicon: G6 = "hand / plural marker", conf=0.90, on Aruku Kurenga
- Secondpass-phase12: G7 "starts sequence of young scouts" — explicitly attributed to Tablet B

### Sequence III Specifically

**G200** (ariki/chief) as the opening glyph — the paramount chief leads. This is confirmed:
- Lexicon: G200 = ariki, conf=0.95, tablets include Aruku Kurenga
- Secondpass-phase14z: "Chief glyphs (ariki) precede personal names in genealogical and leadership contexts"

## The Cave Glyph Question (G013)

The secondpass and main research corpus repeatedly cite a **cave/tomb glyph** appearing *only* in Sequence II to mark the death of scout Kūkū'u. Let's check the lexicon:

**G13 lexicon entry:**
- English meanings include burial/tomb/cave references
- Transliterations: *avanga* (tomb/cave in Rapanui)
- Metoro reportedly said *"avanga"* when shown this glyph in a similar context

The cave glyph (G13) is one of the more compelling narrative-specific identifications in the corpus. Metoro's reading of *avanga* (cave/tomb) in a comparable context, combined with the oral tradition of a scout dying and being buried in a cave at Oromanga, makes this plausible — but the exact confidence rating in the master lexicon should be checked against the raw entry rather than accepted solely from secondary summaries.

## Orientation of Glyph G9 (Sand/Anakena) as Terminal Anchor

G9 (sand/beach/*one*) with confidence **0.95** — the highest-confidence identification on this tablet — appearing at the end of all three sequences is the architectural keystone of Aruku Kurenga's decipherment. Without it, the three-sequence structure remains a formal observation. With it, the narrative becomes readable: **three voyages, one destination.**

The oral tradition, Metoro's readings, the positional evidence, and the lexicon entry all converge on the same identification. This is as close to certainty as Rongorongo gets outside the Mamari full-moon glyph.

---

## Key Findings

1. **10 glyphs** are confirmed on Aruku Kurenga by the master lexicon — enough to establish the narrative skeleton of all three sequences.
2. **G9 (sand/Anakena)** as the universal terminal glyph is the single most important confirmed finding on this tablet.
3. **G7+G6** (child+plural = scouts) and **G200** (ariki = chief) as the sequence-opening glyphs for II and III are confirmed independently in the lexicon.
4. **G32** as sequence separator is confirmed on Aruku Kurenga.
5. Specific place-name glyphs along the migration route are **not confirmed in the lexicon** — they remain interpretive inferences from oral tradition, not direct decipherments.
6. The cave glyph (G13/*avanga*) has Metoro support but needs its lexicon entry checked before being treated as confirmed.
