## The 31-Entry Genealogical Chain

Tablet G contains **31 repeated section markers**, each marking a genealogical entry. This parallels:
- Mamari (C): 30 nights in the lunar calendar
- Keiti (E): 31 adze-glyph occurrences per monthly sequence
- Tablet K: 31 repeated section markers (see below)

**Why 31?** The coincidence of 31 across a genealogical tablet (G), a second genealogical tablet (K), and an astronomical tablet (E) raises a non-trivial question: are all three independently structured around the lunar month count, or is 31 a coincidental feature of their content?

Two non-exclusive possibilities without forcing an answer:
1. **Functional coincidence:** A lunar month has ~30–31 nights, and a standard genealogical chant might recite exactly one lunar month's worth of names in a single performance (one generation per night). The genealogical recitation is timed to the lunar month — 31 names because the chant fills one month.
2. **Historical count:** The genealogy literally records 31 generations of chiefs. If each generation is approximately 25 years, 31 generations = ~775 years — roughly consistent with Rapa Nui's settlement timeline if the first settlement was ~AD 1000–1200 and the genealogy extends to ~AD 1800.

Both explanations are structurally coherent. Neither is confirmed.

This relationship is analogous to the relationship between a complete king-list and an abbreviated summary — the same standard formula applied at different scales.

## What the 31-Entry Structure Means for Cross-Tablet Analysis

The convergence of 31 as a structuring number across three tablets (G genealogical, E astronomical, K genealogical) and the 30-night Mamari calendar sequence raises a genuine research question:

**Is there a deliberate correspondence between the lunar month count and the genealogical entry count?**

In many Polynesian societies, genealogies were deliberately calibrated against calendrical systems — the oral tradition synchronized genealogical chants to lunar months to ensure accurate transmission. A chanter who could recite exactly 30 names in one lunar month's cycle (one name per night) had a built-in mnemonic system for accuracy. 

If Tablet G's 31 genealogical entries are calibrated to the lunar month:
- Each entry = one night
- One complete lunar month's recitation = one complete generation set
- The 31st entry might be an intercalary "extra" name corresponding to the extra night in a 31-day month

This is an emergent hypothesis from the cross-tablet count correspondence — it is not forced, but it is not confirmed. What is confirmed: the number 31 structures both the astronomical and genealogical tablets independently.

7. The **lunar month/genealogical calibration hypothesis** — 31 names timed to 31 nights for mnemonic accuracy — emerges from the cross-tablet count correspondence and is culturally coherent in Polynesian oral tradition, but requires physical transcription data to test
