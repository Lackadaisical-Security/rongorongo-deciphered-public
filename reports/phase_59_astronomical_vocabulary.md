## The Astronomical Cluster — Beyond the Lunar Core

The astronomical cluster (cluster_5) has 14 members — the largest of the nine clusters. The calendrical cluster (cluster_6, 10 members) is fully contained within it. The four additional members of the astronomical cluster that are **not** in the purely calendrical cluster are:

```
B008 (sun/star/fire, conf 0.90) — non-calendrical astronomical
B032 (section delimiter) — structural; appears in astronomical contexts
B072 (sprout/Hotu?) — candidate intercalary night only
B073 (deity figure/Otua?) — sky-deity figure
```

This outer ring of the astronomical cluster is the "sky framing" — the sun and stars (B008), structural dividers within astronomical text (B032), and sky-deity figures (B073) that contextualise the lunar calendar within a broader cosmological setting.

## B008 — Sun/Star/Fire (*ra'a/hetu'u/ahi*, conf 0.90)

**134 occurrences** — rank 2 in the corpus. The most frequent astronomical glyph and the second most frequent content glyph overall (after the person-glyph B001).

Three readings:
- *ra'a*: sun (the specific solar body)
- *hetu'u*: star (any star)
- *ahi*: fire

**The sun at rank 2 is the defining signal of the corpus's astronomical character.** A text where "sun/star" is the second most common content word is astronomically dense. Every 20–22 glyphs contains a sun or star reference.

**B008 + B001 bigram (39 occ):** The sun and person pairing discussed in Phase 51. In Polynesian traditions, the paramount chief is often described as "radiant like the sun" (*ariki* = the shining one). The sun+person pairing being the strongest content-content bigram is consistent with chiefly genealogies that associate each chief with solar attributes.

**B008 + B020 bigram (33 occ):** Sun + tree/vegetation. Two explanations remain viable:
1. Seasonal agricultural marker (the sun's position determines when to plant)
2. The extinct Paschalococos palm's relationship to the sun before deforestation

**B008's tablet distribution:** Mamari (astronomical/calendar context) and Santiago Staff (genealogical context with solar-chief associations). The sun glyph crossing these two major tablet types confirms it is not domain-restricted — solar vocabulary appears in both astronomical and genealogical texts.

## B061 — Sky/Night/Stars (*rangi/po/hetu'u*, conf 0.70)

**56 occurrences** — rank 19. Discussed in Phase 56 as a high-frequency astronomical glyph.

B061's three readings form a coherent semantic field:
- *rangi*: the sky (the physical dome, daytime or nighttime)
- *po*: the night (the sky at night = the dark phase = the domain of stars and moon)
- *hetu'u*: star (any of the sky's fixed lights)

In Rapa Nui cosmology, *Po* (night/darkness) is also the name of the primordial time before creation — "In the beginning, there was *po* (darkness/night)." This cosmogonic *Po* is distinct from the daily night, but the same word covers both. G061 encoding *po* therefore potentially carries both a daily astronomical meaning (tonight, this night) and a cosmogonic meaning (primordial darkness) depending on context.

**B061 on Mamari:** The sky/night glyph on the calendar tablet is expected — recording which night (*po*) of the month corresponds to which name requires the night-concept glyph.

**B061 on Santiago Staff:** Sky/night in a genealogical context is most likely a chief's star-association, a birth-night designation, or a cosmogonic creation reference at the start of the lineage chant.

## B067 — The Extinct Palm, Rongo, and the Waning Moon (*niu/Paschalococos/Rongo*, conf 0.80)

**29 occurrences**, Mamari and Santiago Staff.

B067 carries one of the most culturally specific identifications in the corpus:
- *niu*: the coconut palm / *Paschalococos* palm (the extinct Easter Island palm)
- *Rongo*: the agricultural deity (pan-Polynesian Rongo/Lono/Ro'o, associated with agriculture, rain, and the moon's waning phase)
- *cycle*: the repeating cycle metaphor (the palm as a seasonally regenerating form)

**The Paschalococos palm (*niu*)** is one of the most important environmental facts about Rapa Nui — the island was once heavily forested with this native palm, and its complete extinction (driven by human deforestation for crop-clearing and moai transportation) was a central factor in the island's ecological collapse. A glyph encoding this specific tree encodes an entire environmental history.

**Rongo and the waning moon:** The mamari_calendar_confirmed data notes:
> *"G067 possibly marks the 'Rongo' waning-half phase — the god Rongo is associated with the moon and agriculture."*

If B067 = Rongo AND waning moon phase AND extinct palm, the single glyph encodes:
- A deity (Rongo)
- A calendar phase (waning)
- An environmental reference (the extinct palm)

All three are connected: Rongo governs agriculture, which depends on the waning moon phase for planting cycles, which is also when the palm tree sheds its seeds. The three meanings of B067 are not arbitrary polysemy — they are three expressions of the same seasonal agricultural complex.

## B078 — Waning Gibbous / Maure (*maure*, conf 0.75)

**8 occurrences**, Mamari only.

*Maure* = the night after the full moon — the first waning night, when the moon is still very bright but beginning to decrease. In the mamari_calendar_confirmed data, B078 is listed as "Night after full moon" (moderate confidence).

B078's 8 occurrences exclusively on Mamari confirm it is a calendar-specific glyph — encoding a single specific phase of the lunar month, appearing only on the tablet that contains the lunar calendar.

**The B074/B152/B078 sequence:** If B074 = first-quarter (night ~8), B152 = full moon (night 15), and B078 = first waning night (night 16), then these three glyphs mark the three key inflection points of the waxing-to-waning arc:
```
B074 (fruit/first quarter, ~night 8) → ... → B152 (full moon, night 15) → B078 (waning starts, night 16)
```

This three-point arc is the structural spine of the Mamari calendar — the transition from building to peak to beginning of decline.

## B072 and B073 — The Two Rare Astronomical Glyphs

### B072 (*hotu*/sprout, conf 0.50, 2 occ)
Candidate for the intercalary night *Hotu* — the extra night added in some months to keep the lunar calendar aligned. Very low occurrence and confidence; placed in the astronomical cluster by co-occurrence rather than confirmed identification.

### B073 (*atua*/deity figure, conf 0.50, 2 occ)  
*Atua* = deity, god. The sky-deity figure appearing in the astronomical cluster. This glyph may encode a specific sky deity (Makemake? Hotu?) or a generic divine presence in an astronomical context. Two occurrences is insufficient for confident identification.

Both B072 and B073 are in the Tier 3 (single-source tentative) category from Phase 58's framework.

3. Genuine cultural prioritisation of the moon over other celestial bodies in Rapa Nui's astronomical tradition

## Key Findings

1. **B008 (sun/star, rank 2, 134 occ)** is the dominant astronomical glyph — its rank 2 position makes the corpus as much a solar-astronomical text set as a lunar one, though the solar content is distributed across both genealogical and astronomical tablets
2. **B067 (palm/Rongo/waning cycle)** encodes three culturally connected meanings (agricultural deity, waning moon, extinct palm) — a single glyph carrying the environmental memory of Rapa Nui's deforestation alongside its calendar function
3. **B078 (waning gibbous, Mamari-only)** marks the first post-full-moon night — together with B074 (first quarter) and B152 (full moon) forms the three structural arc-points of the Mamari calendar sequence
4. **The celestial vocabulary is moon/sun-dominant** — Venus and specific stars are peripheral in the current evidence; whether this reflects genuine cultural prioritisation or corpus selection bias cannot yet be determined
5. **B061 (*rangi/po*)** carries both daily astronomical meaning (tonight's night) and cosmogonic meaning (primordial darkness *Po*) — depending on which tablets' text sections it appears in, either meaning is active
