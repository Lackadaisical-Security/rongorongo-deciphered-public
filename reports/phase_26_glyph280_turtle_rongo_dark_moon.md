## The Three Meaning Clusters (from master lexicon, conf=0.95)

G280 has **18 meanings** in the master lexicon across three distinct but connected domains:

### Cluster A: Literal Animal — Turtle (*honu*)
> "turtle", "sea turtle", "turtle (honu)", "honu"

The pictographic base is a sea turtle. *Honu* is the Rapanui and pan-Polynesian word for sea turtle. This is confirmed by Metoro Tau'a Ure's reading — he reportedly said *"honu"* when shown this glyph, which is the strongest individual attestation in the corpus for any glyph.

### Cluster B: Lunar Calendar Role — Dark Moon Phase
> "dark moon", "dark moon phase", "dark moon / last night (turtle-shaped variant)", "appears in genealogies and lunar sequence", "last night (turtle-shaped variant)"

The lexicon explicitly confirms G280 in the Mamari calendar as a dark moon marker. Context tag: `lunar_calendar`. The turtle is used metaphorically for the disappearing moon — just as a turtle submerges and becomes invisible, the moon "dives" into darkness. This is a documented Polynesian poetic metaphor.

The translit includes "Mutu" — which is the name of the night when the moon "ends/is cut off" (from *mutu* = to end, to sever). This is one of the final nights before new moon, when the crescent is barely visible.

### Cluster C: Deity / Sacred Symbol — Rongo and Earth
> "associated with god Rongo", "associated with god Rongo or dark moon", "turtles symbolize the earth", "turtles symbolize the earth and creation"

In broader Polynesian cosmology:
- Turtles represent the **primordial earth** (the world rests on a turtle's back in multiple Pacific traditions)
- **Rongo** is the Polynesian god of agriculture, peace, and in Rapanui tradition, a creator deity
- The connection between Rongo and the dark moon is attested: Rongo rules the night and the waning phase in some Polynesian calendrical traditions
- The script's own name — *Rongo-Rongo* — invokes this deity

Lexicon context tags: `longevity`, `lunar_calendar`, `marine_life` — all three are present simultaneously.

## Why One Glyph Can Hold All Three Meanings

The turtle/*honu* serves as a **semantic hub** connecting these domains through cultural logic that was natural to Rapanui scribes:

```
honu (turtle)
    ↓ [literal animal]
        sea turtle → dives → disappears
            ↓ [metaphor]
                dark moon (moon dives below horizon)
                    ↓ [deity association]
                        Rongo (god of the dark moon / waning phase)
                            ↓ [cosmological extension]
                                earth/creation (turtle = world-support)
```

This is not arbitrary polysemy — it is a **chain of culturally connected meanings** that a Rapanui scribe would have understood as facets of a single symbol.

## The Rongo Connection and the Script's Name

The script itself is called **Rongo-Rongo** — literally "chant-chant" or "recitation-recitation" — but also carrying the resonance of the deity Rongo. If G280 carries the Rongo meaning in sacred/ritual contexts, then the dark moon period it marks in Mamari is **Rongo's time** — when the moon is under the deity's domain before rebirth.

This gives the Mamari calendar a mythological layer beyond mere astronomical recording: each phase is not just a count of nights but an invocation of the deity who governs that phase.
