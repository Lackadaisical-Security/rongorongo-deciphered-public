# Phase 32 — Glyph 20: Plant/Tree/Grow — The Most Overlooked High-Frequency Glyph

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Status:** Complete (Evidence-anchored)

---

## Lexicon Data (conf=0.70, 92 occurrences)

**Meanings:** "'plant' (*tiare*) general vegetation", "action derived from plant symbolism", "branching glyph representing a tree or plant", "grow", "growth sections", *marks agriculture or growth sections*  
**Transliterations:** *rākau, tiare, tipu*  
**Tablets:** Mamari, Santiago Staff, Small Santiago  
**Contexts:** agriculture, botanical, growth, natural  
**Confidence:** 0.70

With **92 occurrences** across three tablets, G20 is the **fourth most frequent glyph in the corpus** (behind G6=156, G8=134, G1=127). Yet it has received less focused attention than the genealogical and calendar glyphs. This phase corrects that.

---

## Why This Glyph Was Underweighted

G20 is visually described as a "branching glyph" — a branching/tree shape that at first appears similar to G67 (extinct palm) and G143 (Rākau/tree, calendar). The visual similarity to other vegetation glyphs may have caused researchers to undercount its distinctive role.

The key distinguishing feature: G20 has **three different transliterations**:
- *rākau* = wood/tree (shared with G143)
- *tiare* = flower/plant (a generic vegetation term)
- *tipu* = to grow, to sprout (a **verb**)

The verbal transliteration *tipu* is crucial — G20 can function as a **verb of growth** not just a noun of vegetation. This makes it one of the few confirmed verbal glyphs in the corpus alongside G76 (copulate/beget) and G62 (clause break/ki).

---

## G20 in Agricultural Context — Tablet Content

The lexicon note says G20 "marks agriculture or growth sections." On the Santiago Staff and Small Santiago, G20 appearing in clusters likely marks sections of text dealing with:
- Plant cultivation, crops
- Seasonal agricultural cycles
- Rituals for ensuring growth (invoking Rongo, using the rain-lizard G69)

The secondpass-phase12 describes a passage on "Tablet A" (Tahua) interpreted as a yam-planting prayer:
> *"(Oh) Rongo, make the rain pour, make the yam sprout..."*

If G20 (grow/*tipu*) appears in that passage, it is the **verb** in this prayer: "cause to grow." The prayer structure would then be:

> G280 (Rongo) + G8 (sun/fire) + G69 (rain/lizard) + **G20** (grow/*tipu*) + [crop glyph] = "Rongo, with fire and rain, make [the crop] grow"

---

## G20 on Mamari — Agricultural Calendar Layer

Mamari is primarily known as a lunar calendar, but the calendar section serves a **practical agricultural purpose**: telling farmers when to plant, water, and harvest by the moon. G20 on Mamari likely marks the agricultural interpretation of each lunar phase:

- Waxing moon (pre-G74 first quarter): planting phase → G20 (*grow/plant*)
- Post-full moon (G67 waning marker): harvesting phase → G20 (*harvest/mature plant*)

The presence of G20 on Mamari means the calendar is not purely astronomical — it has embedded agricultural instructions. This is consistent with Polynesian lunar calendars globally, which always link moon phases to cultivation timing.

---

## G20 vs G67 vs G143 — Three Vegetation Glyphs Distinguished

| Glyph | Primary | Context | When to use which |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **G20** | Plant/grow (*tiaru/tipu*) | Agricultural, general vegetation, growth verb | When the text is about cultivation, crops, or growth processes |
| **G67** | Extinct palm (*Paschalococos*) / cycle | Waning moon phase marker; cycle; Rongo deity | When marking the waning half of the lunar cycle or invoking Rongo |
| **G143** | Rākau (tree/wood) — night before full moon | Specific Mamari calendar night | Only in the pre-full-moon position of the Mamari calendar |

Three glyphs, all branching/tree-like visually, but used in completely different situations. A reading that confuses them will generate incoherent translations.

---

## G30 (House/*hare*) — Related High-Frequency Glyph Noted

G30 (conf=0.65, 73 occurrences, Santiago Staff + Small Santiago) — house/dwelling/*hare*. Also means *henua* = land, place. With 73 occurrences, G30 is another high-frequency glyph that suggests the texts include concrete descriptions of settlement, place, and habitat — not just abstract genealogy and cosmology. The *henua* reading ("land") is particularly significant: *ariki henua* = "paramount chief of the land" contains G200 (ariki/chief) + G30 (henua/land) as a title compound.

---

## Key Findings

1. G20 is the **fourth most frequent glyph** in the corpus at 92 occurrences — it is not a minor sign.
2. Its verbal transliteration *tipu* (to grow) makes G20 one of the confirmed **verb glyphs** alongside G76 and G62.
3. G20 on Mamari adds an **agricultural layer** to the lunar calendar — moon phases linked to cultivation timing.
4. Three vegetation glyphs (G20, G67, G143) are visually similar but contextually distinct: growth/crops (G20), waning-moon cycle/Rongo (G67), night-before-full-moon (G143).
5. **G30** (house/land, 73 occurrences) is another overlooked high-frequency glyph — the *ariki henua* title compound likely uses G200+G30 together.
